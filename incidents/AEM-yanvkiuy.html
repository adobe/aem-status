Postmortem

On October 20, 2025 at 07:07 UTC, we detected error messages in our monitoring system related to an underlying AWS outage in the US-EAST-1 region. AWS reported increased error rates and latencies affecting multiple services, with the root cause identified as DNS resolution issues with the DynamoDB API endpoint.

The incident initially impacted AEM publishing operations. At 07:20 UTC, we began observing a low level of delivery errors, totaling approximately 200 errors. Our delivery infrastructure operates as a dual-stack system across multiple regions, which allowed our team to quickly switch to the secondary stack at 07:25 UTC, eliminating delivery disruptions while continuing to investigate the publishing impact.

At the time of the incident, publishing was operating on a single-stack, single-region configuration. At 09:03 UTC, our team successfully brought up a secondary region for AEM publishing, restoring full publishing functionality even as the AWS incident continued. By 12:06 UTC, we began restoring the original service configuration while closely monitoring the situation. Normal operations were fully restored at 12:14 UTC, with a total incident duration of 5 hours and 7 minutes.

The rapid mitigation demonstrated the effectiveness of our dual-stack delivery architecture for immediate failover, and our team's ability to rapidly provision and deploy additional infrastructure capacity for publishing during an active incident. This allowed us to maintain service continuity despite the severity of the underlying AWS infrastructure issue that affected numerous major platforms globally.
